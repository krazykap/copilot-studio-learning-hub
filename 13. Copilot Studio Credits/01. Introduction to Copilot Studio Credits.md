## What Are Copilot Studio Credits?
At the core of Copilot Studio’s economic model lies a concept called Copilot Credits (a unit of consumption) - a unified, consumption-based unit that measures how much “work” an AI agent performs. In simple terms, **Copilot Credits are the currency that powers every interaction inside Copilot Studio**.

Copilot Credit is a digital token that is drawn down every time your agent performs an action that requires compute or AI processing. Think of it like a prepaid SIM card: you load credits into your Microsoft tenant, and your agents spend those credits as users interact with them. Credits are the mechanism Microsoft uses to measure and meter the usage of AI capabilities within Copilot Studio.

<pre>
<b>💡 Simple Analogy</b>
Imagine your organisation runs a fleet of company cars. Rather than charging each driver a fixed monthly lease (traditional licensing), you switch to a fuel-card model where each driver is allocated a fuel budget and is charged only for the kilometres they actually drive.

Copilot Studio Credits work in exactly the same way — you pay for the AI "fuel" your agents consume, not for seats that may sit idle.
</pre>

Agent consumes **Copilot Credits** whenever it performs an action that requires AI processing. This includes:
- Respond to user query
- Retrives knowledge (RAG Scenarios)
- Executes an actionn on behalf of the user (e.g. book a meeting, send an email) - Power Automate, Connectors, APIs
- Invokes AI Models or tools

### The Credit Lifecycle at a Glance
Understanding credits begins with recognising their simple lifecycle within your Microsoft tenant:
1. **Provisioned** — Credits are allocated either through your Microsoft 365 / Dynamics 365 license entitlements or via purchased add-on packs.
2. **Pooled —** Credits are held at the tenant level and can be distributed across environments (Production, UAT, Development).
3. **Consumed —** Credits are drawn in real time as agents respond to user messages, invoke AI capabilities, or execute actions.
4. **Monitored —** Usage is tracked through the Power Platform Admin Center and Copilot Studio Analytics.
5. **Renewed —** Credits are topped up at the start of each billing cycle or on demand via add-on purchases.

## Why Credits Matter?
For years, we have been accustomed to traditional licensing models where they pay for a fixed number of seats or instances. While this model provides predictability, it often leads to overprovisioning (paying for unused capacity) or underprovisioning (running out of licenses during peak demand). Copilot Studio Credits introduce a new paradigm that aligns cost with actual usage, offering several key benefits:

However, with great power comes great responsibility. The consumption-based model requires a new mindset around cost management and optimisation. It is no longer sufficient to simply provision licenses; you must actively monitor and manage credit usage to ensure your agents are delivering value without breaking the bank.

AI workloads are fundamentally different. An AI agent does not get used the same way every day. A customer service bot may see spikes in usage during certain hours, while an internal HR assistant may have more consistent but lower volume interactions. The consumption-based model allows you to pay for what you use, but it also means you need to be vigilant about monitoring and optimising that usage.

### Traditional Licensing vs. Consumption-Based Credits
| Aspect | Traditional Licensing | Consumption-Based Credits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cost Model | Fixed monthly/annual fee per user or instance | Pay-as-you-go based on actual usage (credits) |
| Flexibility | Limited flexibility; often requires overprovisioning | High flexibility; scale up or down based on demand |
| Ideal for | Stable, predictable workloads with consistent usage | Variable workloads with fluctuating demand |
| Overprovisioning Risk | High (paying for unused licenses) | Low (pay only for what you use) |
| Ideal Cost | Predictable but potentially higher due to overprovisioning | Potentially lower if optimised, but can spike if not monitored |
| Visibility | Limited visibility into actual usage patterns | Real-time visibility into consumption and costs |
| Governance | License-based governance (e.g., who has access) | Consumption-based governance (e.g., who can consume credits) |

## How Credits differ from Traditonal Copilot Studio Licensing
If you have been working on Copilot Studio for a while, you may be familiar with the concept of licensing - where you pay for a certain number of seats or instances that can access the platform. While licensing is still relevant for accessing the platform itself, **credits are the currency that powers the actual usage of AI capabilities within Copilot Studio**.

it has evolved into more unified and flexible consumption-based model that better aligns with the variable nature of AI workloads. In this model, you provision a pool of credits at the tenant level, and your agents draw from that pool as they perform actions that require AI processing. This shift from licensing to credits represents a fundamental change in how we think about cost and value in the world of AI agents.

### 3 Licensing Pillars
1. **Microsoft 365 and Dynamics 365 Entitlements:** Many Microsoft 365 and Dynamics 365 licenses now include a certain number of Copilot Studio Credits as part of their entitlements. This means that if your organisation already has these licenses, you may have access to a baseline level of credits without needing to purchase additional add-ons.
2. **Copilot Studio Standalone Subscription:** For organisations that require more credits than what is included in their Microsoft 365 or Dynamics 365 licenses, Microsoft offers standalone Copilot Studio subscriptions. These subscriptions provide a dedicated pool of credits that can be used across all your agents and environments.
3. **Add-On Credit Packs:** If you find that your agents are consuming credits faster than anticipated, you can purchase additional credit packs on demand. This allows you to scale your credit pool up or down based on actual usage patterns, providing maximum flexibility and control over your costs.

## Where Credits Fit in the Power Platform Ecosystem?
Copilot Studio does not exist in isolation; it is part of the broader Power Platform ecosystem that includes Power Apps, Power Automate, Power BI, and Power Virtual Agents. Understanding how credits fit into this ecosystem is crucial for architects and makers who are building integrated solutions that span multiple components.

### Service Interactions That Affect Credit Consumption
- Power Automate Flows: If your agent triggers a Power Automate flow that performs actions on behalf of the user (e.g., sending an email, creating a calendar event), those actions may also consume credits depending on the complexity and AI involvement. However, the agent's initial trigger and response handling still draw on Copilot Studio credits.
- AI Builder: If your agent uses AI Builder models for tasks like sentiment analysis, object detection, or form processing, those interactions will also consume credits. The more complex the AI processing, the more credits may be consumed.
- Azure Open AI Service: If your agent invokes Azure Open AI Service for advanced language understanding or generation capabilities, those interactions will consume credits based on the amount of compute and AI processing required.
- Microsoft Dataverse: Knowledge retrieval scenarios that involve querying Microsoft Dataverse may also consume credits, especially if they involve complex queries or large datasets.

## Summary
Before diving into technical detail of tokenisation and credit consumption in the next chapter, it is important to understand the fundamental shift that Copilot Studio Credits represent in how we think about cost and value in AI agent development. Credits are not just a billing mechanism; they are a new paradigm that requires a new mindset around cost management, optimisation, and governance.

### Key Concepts to Remember:
**🧠 Design is Cost Engineering:** Every design decision you make in your agent - from the complexity of the AI processing to the frequency of user interactions - has cost implications. As architects and makers, you must now think like cost engineers, designing not just for functionality but also for efficiency and sustainability.

**📊 Visibility Enables Control:** You cannot govern what you cannot see. The Admin Center dashboards and analytics tools are not optional extras — they are the control room for your AI estate. Learning to read them fluently is a critical skill.

**⚖️ Credits Are Shared Resources:** In most organisations, Copilot Studio credits are pooled at the tenant level. This means a runaway agent in one environment can starve critical agents in another. Governance is not bureaucracy — it is protection.
