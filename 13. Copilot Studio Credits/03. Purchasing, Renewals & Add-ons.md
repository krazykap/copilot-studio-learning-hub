### Understanding Each Rate Category
**Classic Answer - 1 Credit**

These are static responses that do not involve any generative AI processing. They are predefined and manually authored, making them the most cost-effective option for simple queries.

**Generative Answer - 2 Credits**

Generative answers are AI-generated responses based on user queries. They involve natural language processing and generation, which is why they consume more credits than classic answers.

**Agent Action (Tool Call) - 5 Credits**

Agent actions involve orchestrating a tool call, such as triggering deep reasoning, topic transitions, invoking an API, or executing a workflow. These actions require additional processing beyond generating a response, which is reflected in the higher credit cost.

**Tenant Graph Grounding - 10 Credits**

Tenant graph grounding involves grouping conversations based on tenant graph data to provide contextually relevant responses. This process is more complex and resource-intensive, leading to a higher credit cost.

**Agent Flow Actions (per 100 actions) - 13 Credits**

Agent flow actions are predefined action sequences that do not involve LLM orchestration at each step. They provide a structured way to manage complex interactions, which is why they have a specific credit cost.

**AI tools Premium - 100 credits per 10 responses**

When an agent uses a reasoning-capable language model, Copilot Studio bills by using two billing meters: the feature rate and text and generative AI tools (premium). The feature rate covers the core action the agent performs. The text and generative AI tools (premium) rate applies when the agent uses a reasoning model, covering the extra computational resources required for deep reasoning, planning, and multistep inference. At 100 credits per 10 responses (10 credits per response), premium AI tool usage is the highest-cost tier and applies exclusively when reasoning-capable models are invoked.

## License Tiers and Their Credit Entitlements
### Tier 1: Microsoft 365 Copilot License — $30/user/month
The Coplit Studio lite experience is included at no additional cost for users with a Microsoft 365 Copilot license at $30/user/month. This means that any user with this license can interact with agents and generate responses without incurring credit costs for their interactions. However, it's important to note that while the license allows for free interactions, the underlying credit consumption still occurs in the background. The license essentially subsidizes the cost of credits for these users, but organizations should still monitor overall credit usage to manage costs effectively. Agents built for Microsoft Teams, SharePoint, and Microsoft 365 Copilot are included at no extra charge.

| Capability | Included at No Extra Credit Cost |
|------------|----------------------------------|
| Classic answer in M365 channels | ✅ Included |
| Generative answer in M365 channels | ✅ Included |
| Agent actions in M365 channels | ✅ Included |
| Tenant graph grounding in M365 channels | ✅ Included |
| Agent Flow actions in M365 channels | ✅ Included |
| External channels (e.g., custom apps, third-party integrations) | ❌ Not Included (credits required) |
| Autonomous agents triggered externally | ❌ Not Included (credits required) |

**Limitaations and Considerations**
- The no-cost credit usage applies only to interactions that occur within Microsoft 365 channels (e.g., Teams, SharePoint, and Microsoft 365 Copilot). If agents are used in external channels or through custom integrations, credit costs will apply as per the official billing rates.
- While the license covers the credit costs for interactions, organizations should still monitor overall credit consumption to ensure they stay within budget and manage their credit pool effectively. High usage of generative answers, agent actions, or tenant graph grounding can lead to increased credit consumption, even if individual interactions are subsidized by the license.
- Organizations should also consider the scale of their deployment. If a large number of users are interacting with agents frequently, the cumulative credit consumption can become significant, and additional credits may need to be purchased to maintain service continuity.
- It's essential to regularly review and analyze credit usage patterns to optimize agent design and user interactions, ensuring that the benefits of Copilot Studio are maximized while keeping costs manageable.
- Finally, while the Microsoft 365 Copilot license provides a cost-effective way to enable agent interactions for users, organizations should also consider the overall value and ROI of their Copilot Studio deployment, balancing the benefits of enhanced productivity and user experience against the costs of credit consumption.

### Tier 2: Microsoft 365 / Dynamics 365 Subscription Entitlements
For users without a Microsoft 365 Copilot license, credit consumption will occur according to the official billing rates for each agent capability. However, these users may still have access to a default allocation of credits based on their subscription entitlements. The specific credit entitlements can vary based on the type of Microsoft 365 or Dynamics 365 subscription, and organizations should refer to their licensing agreements or consult with their Microsoft representative to understand the exact allocations. Qualifying Microsoft 365 and Dynamics 365 plans include a base credit allocation as part of the subscription. These are modest allocations - designed for light, internal use cases rather than production-grade enterprise deployments.

| Licesne Plan | Typical Use Case Coverage |
|--------------|--------------------------|
| Microsoft 365 E3 | Light internal use, experimentation, and pilot projects. Not intended for production workloads. |
| Microsoft 365 E5 | Moderate internal use with some production workloads. Suitable for teams exploring agent capabilities. |
| Dynamics 365 Sales | Light use within sales scenarios, such as lead generation and customer insights. Not intended for heavy agent usage. |
| Dynamics 365 Customer Service | Light use within customer service scenarios, such as case management and knowledge base interactions. Not intended for heavy agent usage. |

**The entitlement gap:** For any deployment beyond light internal use, subscription entitlements alone will be insufficient. Model the gap between your entitlement allocation and your actual production requirement within the first 30 days of deployment - not after credits run out.

## Pricing Models: Pay-as-you-go, Packs, Pre-Purchased Credits
Microsoft offers flexible purchasing options for Copilot Studio credits to accommodate different organizational needs and usage patterns. The three primary pricing models are Pay-as-you-go, Packs, and Pre-Purchased Credits. Each model has its own advantages and considerations, allowing organizations to choose the one that best fits their budget and usage requirements.

### Copilot Studio Packs
Copilot Studio Packs allow organizations to purchase credits in predefined bundles at a discounted rate compared to Pay-as-you-go pricing. These packs provide a cost-effective option for organizations with predictable usage patterns, as they can save money by purchasing credits in bulk. The specific pack sizes and discounts may vary, so it's important to review the available options and choose the one that aligns with your expected credit consumption.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Pricing | $200/month per pack |
| Commitment | Monthly subscription with a fixed number of credits; auto-renews each month |
| Credits included | Each pack includes 25,000 credits |
| Effective rate per credit | $0.008 per credit (20% discount compared to Pay-as-you-go) |
| Stacking | Multiple packs can be purchased to increase credit capacity; credits do not roll over month-to-month |
| Best for | Organizations with predictable and consistent credit usage; those who want to save money compared to Pay-as-you-go |

<div style="background-color: #f0fff4; padding: 15px; border-left: 5px solid #28a745; margin-bottom: 10px;">
  <strong>The pack pricing advantage</strong><br /> 
  At $0.008 per credit, packs are 20% cheaper than PAYG ($0.01/credit). For any deployment with predictable monthly volume, packs consistently outperform PAYG on a cost-per-credit basis.
</div>

### Pay-as-you-go
With Copilot Studio's Pay-as-you-go model, organizations are billed based on their actual credit consumption at a rate of $0.01 per credit. This model offers maximum flexibility, allowing organizations to scale their usage up or down without any upfront commitment. It's ideal for organizations that are just starting with Copilot Studio or have unpredictable usage patterns.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Pricing | $0.01 per credit |
| Commitment | No upfront commitment; pay based on actual usage |
| Effective rates vs packs/pre-purchased | Can be more expensive than packs or pre-purchased credits for high usage scenarios |
| Setup requirement | Active Azure Subscription with Pay-as-you-go enabled; link to Power Platform environment via billing policy |
| Best for | Organizations with low or unpredictable usage; those who want to start without upfront costs |
| Risk factors | Potential for higher costs if usage spikes unexpectedly; requires active monitoring of credit consumption |

<div style="background-color: #f0fff4; padding: 15px; border-left: 5px solid #28a745; margin-bottom: 10px;">
  <strong>PAYG Governance Safeguard</strong> <br />
  If PAYG is enabled, configure Azure Cost Management budget alerts on the linked Azure subscription before it goes live. Without a spending ceiling, a runaway autonomous agent, an unexpected viral adoption event, or a development team accidentally testing against a production environment can generate Azure charges far exceeding budget expectations. Treat PAYG as an overflow valve with a defined pressure limit - not an open tap.
</div>

### Copilot Credit Pre-Purchase Plan (P3)
The Pre-Purchase Plan (P3) allows organizations to buy credits in advance at a discounted rate, providing cost savings for those with predictable and high-volume usage. This model is ideal for organizations that have a clear understanding of their credit needs and want to secure a large number of credits at a lower cost.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Pricing | Custom pricing based on volume; typically offers the lowest cost per credit |
| Commitment | Annual commitment with a large upfront purchase of credits |
| Discount | Up to 20% vs monthly pack pricing (tiered — larger commitments = higher discount) |
| Credit Pool | Copilot Credit Commit Units (CCCUs) - shared across Copilot Studio, D365 agents, and Copilot Chat |
| Renewal | Annual renewal with potential for renegotiation based on usage and future needs |
| Setup requirement | Azure portal (requires Azure subscription) or through Microsoft account team |
| Best for | Large enterprises with predictable, high-volume credit needs; those who want to maximize cost savings |

## Pricing Model Comparison: Which One is Right for You?
When choosing between Pay-as-you-go, Packs, and Pre-Purchased Credits, organizations should consider their expected credit consumption, budget constraints, and willingness to commit to a specific purchasing model. Here's a quick comparison to help guide the decision:

| Attribute | Pay-as-you-go | Packs | Pre-Purchased Credits (P3) |
|-----------|---------------|-------|----------------------------|
| Cost per credit | $0.01 | $0.008 (20% discount) | Custom pricing with up to 20% discount vs packs |
| Commitment | None | Monthly subscription | Annual commitment with upfront purchase |
| Flexibility | High | Moderate (monthly commitment) | Low (annual commitment) |
| Best for | Unpredictable usage; those new to Copilot Studio | Predictable monthly usage; cost-conscious organizations | Large enterprises with high, predictable usage; those seeking maximum savings |
| Risk factors | Potential for high costs if usage spikes; requires monitoring | Monthly commitment may lead to unused credits if usage drops | Large upfront cost; risk of overcommitting if usage is overestimated |

| Situation | Recommended Model | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Just starting with Copilot Studio and want to test the waters | Pay-as-you-go | No upfront commitment; pay based on actual usage |
| Predictable monthly usage with a clear understanding of credit needs | Packs | Cost-effective option with a 20% discount compared to Pay-as-you-go |
| Small internal agent predictable ~25,000 credits/month | 1 Pack | Provides a cost-effective way to manage predictable usage with a 20% discount |
| Moderate production agent with ~100,000 credits/month | Monthly Packs (2-10) | Allows for scaling credit capacity with multiple packs while maintaining cost savings |
| Enterprise deployment with high and predictable credit needs (e.g., 1 million credits/month) | Pre-Purchased Credits (P3) | Offers the best cost savings for large enterprises with high and predictable credit needs, albeit with an annual commitment |

In summary, the choice of pricing model depends on your organization's specific needs and circumstances. Pay-as-you-go offers flexibility but can be costly for high usage, while Packs provide a cost-effective option for predictable usage. Pre-Purchased Credits (P3) offer the best cost savings for large enterprises with high and predictable credit needs but require an annual commitment. Assess your expected credit consumption, budget, and willingness to commit to determine the best option for your organization.

## 4- tier Environment Strategy: Managing Credits Across Multiple Tenants and Environments
In larger organizations, it's common to have multiple environments (e.g., development, testing, production) and even multiple tenants. Managing credits across these environments can be complex, but with a strategic approach, you can ensure that each environment has the necessary credits while maintaining control over overall consumption.

**Tier 1: Production Environment: Protect First**
- Largest credit allocation to ensure uninterrupted service for end-users
- Real time monitoring and alerts (threshold 70% and 90% for credit consumption) to prevent outages

**Tier 2: UAT/Staging Environment: Mirror Production**
- Moderate credit allocation to support realistic testing and validation of agents before they go live
- Regular reviews of credit usage to ensure it aligns with testing needs without over-provisioning

**Tier 3: Development Environment: Cap for Safety**
- Smaller credit allocation to support development and experimentation while preventing runaway costs
- Strict monitoring and alerts to catch unexpected spikes in credit consumption, which can occur during development and testing of new agents or features

**Tier 4: Sandbox/Experimentation Environment: Encourage Innovation with Guardrails**
- Minimal credit allocation to allow for experimentation and innovation while maintaining cost control
- Encourage developers and teams to test new ideas and agent configurations, but with strict monitoring and alerts to prevent excessive credit consumption

## Bringing it All Together: Strategic Credit Management for Copilot Studio Success
Effective credit management is crucial for the success of your Copilot Studio deployment. By understanding the official billing rates, choosing the right pricing model, and strategically managing credits across multiple environments, you can ensure that your agents deliver value while keeping costs under control. Regular monitoring, analysis of credit consumption patterns, and proactive adjustments to your credit strategy will help you maximize the benefits of Copilot Studio while maintaining financial sustainability.
